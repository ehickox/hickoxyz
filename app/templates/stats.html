Let x equal the average number of commits per week in the first quarter of Eli's professional career. From there, you can calculate how many times more productive he has become. This calculation is done in search of the fabled "10x engineer," the engineer who can operate at an order of magnitude more than the average operational capacity. Note there is no universally agreed upon way to calculate this mythical number and this is more of a fun exercise to learn a charting library. In other words, don't take this number too seriously.
